For a while I have had a filter set up in Gmail that automatically labels anything that is related to an order or tracking. I felt like this may be something useful for others so here it is

<!--more-->

### Open up Gmail in your favorite browser(If you are about to click on that blue e then read [this](http://tygertown.us/2009/04/save-time-using-free-stuff/ "Save time using free stuff") first).

### I&#8217;ll be using <a title="Google Chrome Browser" href="http://www.google.com/chrome" target="_blank">Google Chrome</a>Â for this tutorial

&nbsp;

### Click on the Gear in the top right and then click setting&#8217;s
  
[<img class="size-full wp-image-910 alignnone" title="GmailSettings" src="http://tygertown.us/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/GmailSettings.png" alt="" width="383" height="276" />](http://tygertown.us/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/GmailSettings.png)

### 

### Click on the filters tab at the top
  
[<img class=" wp-image-908 alignnone" title="GmailFilters" src="http://162.243.87.189/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/gmailfilters.png" alt="" width="371" height="540" />](http://162.243.87.189/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/gmailfilters.png)

&nbsp;

&nbsp;

### Scroll all the way to the bottom of the page so you can see the links &#8220;Create a new filter&#8221; and &#8220;Import filters&#8221;[<img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-916" title="CreateImportButtons" src="http://tygertown.us/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/CreateImportButtons.png" alt="" width="1018" height="270" />](http://tygertown.us/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/CreateImportButtons.png)

&nbsp;

### You now have the choice of importing existing filters that have been saved by somebody else, or you can create your own by clicking Create a new filter. For this tutorial I&#8217;m going to show you how to create your own and I will link the filter we create so you can import it easily if you want at the end. Click Create a new filter.

[<img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-917" title="FilterDialogue" src="http://tygertown.us/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/FilterDialogue.png" alt="" width="678" height="350" />](http://tygertown.us/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/FilterDialogue.png)

&nbsp;

### Here you can specify different criteria that Gmail will use to filter email when it arrives in your account. For this tutorial we will paste the following into the &#8220;Has the words&#8221; field

> &#8220;track your package&#8221; OR &#8220;tracking number&#8221; OR shipment OR &#8220;order number&#8221; OR &#8220;Receipt for your PayPal payment&#8221; OR &#8220;You bought this item on ebay&#8221; OR &#8220;Receipt for Your Payment&#8221; OR &#8220;Your Order with&#8221; OR &#8220;Your Amazon.com order&#8221;

[<img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-919" title="HasTheWords" src="http://tygertown.us/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/HasTheWords-300x184.png" alt="" width="300" height="184" />](http://tygertown.us/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/HasTheWords.png)

&nbsp;

### Click Create filter with this search

You now get a dialogue box that allows you to specify what to do with email that matches the rules you defined in the last dialogue box

[<img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-920" title="WhatToDo" src="http://tygertown.us/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/WhatToDo-300x209.png" alt="" width="300" height="209" />](http://tygertown.us/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/WhatToDo.png)

  1. Check the box Apply the label
  2. Click Choose label
  3. [<img class="alignnone size-thumbnail wp-image-921" title="SelectNewLabel" src="http://tygertown.us/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/SelectNewLabel-150x150.png" alt="" width="150" height="150" />](http://tygertown.us/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/SelectNewLabel.png)
  4. Give your label a name and click Create
  5. [<img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-922" title="CreateNewLabel" src="http://tygertown.us/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/CreateNewLabel-300x133.png" alt="" width="300" height="133" />](http://tygertown.us/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/CreateNewLabel.png)
  6. Check the &#8220;Also apply filter to&#8230;.&#8221;
  7. Click Create filter

### Your filter should now be created and applied to all existing matches as well. You can verify it by looking on the left side of the Gmail window and clicking on y0ur new label
